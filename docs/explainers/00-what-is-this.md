# What Is This?

This is a **Prisoner's Dilemma tournament platform**, built by the L&G Asset Management AI Club as a fortnightly vibe-coding challenge.

You're looking at a site where small programs — "bots" — compete against each other by playing one of the most famous games in mathematics: the *Prisoner's Dilemma*. Each bot follows a strategy (cooperate? defect? retaliate? forgive?) and earns points based on how its choices interact with its opponents'.

## What can I do here?

- **Watch the arena** — the landing page drops you straight into a live simulation. Bots wander around a map of 1 Coleman Street (our London office), bump into each other, and play rounds of the Prisoner's Dilemma. Green lines mean mutual cooperation; red means someone defected. Click a bot to inspect its strategy and history.

- **Run a tournament** — switch to the Tournament tab to pit bots against each other in a formal round-robin or evolutionary tournament. Pick which bots to include, how many copies of each, and watch the results.

- **Learn the theory** — you're reading it now. These pages walk you through the game, the strategies, and the surprisingly deep ideas behind a two-choice game that has occupied mathematicians, biologists, and philosophers for decades.

## Why the Prisoner's Dilemma?

Because it's the simplest possible model of a question that matters everywhere: *when should you cooperate, and when should you look out for yourself?*

The answer turns out to be richer than you'd expect. In a single encounter, selfishness wins. But when the game repeats — when there's a "shadow of the future" — strategies that build trust tend to come out on top. That tension between short-term temptation and long-term cooperation is what makes the Prisoner's Dilemma endlessly fascinating.
